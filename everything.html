Dear Hosteller:

Thank you for inquiring about former Pittsburgh International Hostel. We have put together this fact sheet that we hope will answer most of your questions prior to arrival. If you still have questions after you’ve read this, please let us know!

What Is A Hostel?

The spirit of hostelling is to get out and explore the world. Hostels are comfortable, environmentally sensitive places for budget-minded independent travelers to lodge for the night and provide dormitory-style accommodations, women in one room, men in the other. (See below for information about private/family rooms). Bathroom facilities are provided in each dorm, as are lockers. Meet people from all over the world when using the common area, do-it-yourself kitchen and dining room. Just like at home, hostellers clean up after themselves. With everyone pitching in, the place is spotless in no time and the hostel can keep the overnight charge as low as possible. Hostels provide the bed, pillow and blanket – you bring your own sheets, pillowcase, locks and towels, or rent them upon arrival.

Location:

The hostel was conveniently located in the Allentown area of Pittsburgh two miles south of downtown above the historic Southside between Mt. Oliver and Mt. Washington. See below for directions.

Hours:

For 2000, Hostel Hours were: 0800-1000 and 1700-2200. The former Pittsburgh International Hostel on Warrington was NOT a 24-hour-a-day facility.

The hostel was closed every year for the nights of December 23rd through December 26th for Christmas.

We were closed during the day. That’s when you get to explore Pittsburgh! Guests return to the hostel after 5 p.m. Lockers and visitor information were available when the hostel was closed.

All guests must check-in no later than 2200 (10 p.m.). Sorry no exceptions.

Facilities:

The 50-bed hostel was located in a renovated 19th century bank building, complete with 8-ton vault door (now the entrance to our elevator). The building was fully handicapped accessible. Women and men were accommodated in separate spacious dormitory-style rooms with bathroom facilities located in each. A limited number of family rooms were available (see below). The top floor of the hostel contains: a fabulous kitchen that inspires cooks and conversation, a dining room seating 25-30, two common rooms, and a conference/class room with a TV and VCR. We also provide free parking on our lot just up the street from the hostel.

Prices:

For individual guests in dormitory-style accommodation. (All prices include tax.)
  • Hostelling International members: $17.00/person/night.
  • Non-members may stay for an additional $3.00/night.
  • Memberships were available on the spot.
  • Children ages 5-17 were $8.50, ages 4 and under were free.

    • Supervision policies regarding children:

  • Infants – 4 years: family room only in consideration of our other guests
  • 5-11 years: may in stay in dormitory with same gender chaperone over age 18
  • 12-17 years: may stay in dormitory alone with chaperone over 18 in the building
  • Call before arrival to confirm availability of family rooms (for which a surcharge applies), if appropriate.
  • All children under 18 must have an adult chaperone of the appropriate gender.

    • Family Rooms:

    A limited number of private/family rooms were available. Reservations were essential for family rooms (especially for Friday and Saturday nights). A surcharge of $10.00/night applies in addition to member/non-member/child rates (see above) and includes, in addition to the private room, all linen and a semi-private bathroom across the hall.

    NOTE: Minimum charge and key deposit apply to family rooms. $50 minimum for family rooms containing one double bed and one set of bunks. Final price may be higher than these minimum charges and was based on member/non-member/child rates plus the nightly surcharge. Check availability of these rooms for the nights you need by contacting the hostel.

    Groups:

    Hostels are great for groups of all kinds! Scout and school groups, family reunions, overflow accommodation when it’s full at your place! Groups consist of 10 or more people arriving together and are welcomed with advanced reservations. Please inquire about our discounted group rate. We prefer that groups contact us no less than one month in advance to ensure bed availability. Due to the layout of our building, normally only 15 people of each gender can be accommodated within a group. Meals (breakfast and/or lunch) can be arranged for groups with advanced notice and for a small additional charge per person. Please ask for our Group Inquiry Packet.

    Day Use:

    Rental of the hostel during the day when the hostel was closed was available for meetings, conferences, receptions, classes, family reunions, etc., (with or without overnight accommodation). Please contact the hostel for details, availability and pricing. Affordable for-profit and non-profit day use rates make the hostel an ideal place to hold your event while supporting Hostelling International (a non-profit organization). Please ask for our Day Use Agreement.

    Additional Information:

    Don’t forget to bring your membership card! The membership rate was available only upon presentation of a valid Hostelling International membership card at check-in.

    Because hostels are intended for travelers and are not permanent housing, we have a length of stay limit.

    Government-issued photo ID required to check-in: Passport, driver’s license, state or military ID.

    Sleeping bags were NOT allowed in our hostel for sanitary reasons.

    Each bed was supplied with a pillow and blanket. You provide the linen, either by bringing your own or renting it from us for a small charge ($1.00 for sleep sacks, $.50 for towels)

    We have a 24-hour cancellation policy. Your credit card will be billed for the first night’s deposit if you fail to cancel your reservation by 10 p.m. the night before your intended arrival date.

    Near the Hostel:

    The following are within walking distance of the hostel: bank with ATM machine, gas station, 2 markets, 1 grocery store, a few bars and restaurants, and most other essentials. East Carson Street (the main street of the historic Southside which is lined with bars and restaurants, etc.) and scenic Grandview Avenue on Mt. Washington and its famous inclines (vertical railways) are also within walking distance.
